East Africa Demographic Overview

The East African region represents one of the world’s most dynamic and fast-growing consumer markets. With a combined population exceeding 315 million people.

East Africa Demographic Highlights population Range: 11M (South Sudan) to 68M (Tanzania) Median Age: Very young populations—mostly between 16.5 and 20 years Urbanization: Generally low, ranging from 14% (Burundi) to 37% (Tanzania) Mobile Penetration: Highest in Kenya and Rwanda (87–90%+), lowest in South Sudan (~35%) Internet Usage: Rwanda leads (~60%), while Burundi and South Sudan lag (~8–11%) GDP Growth: Strongest in Rwanda and Uganda (6–8%), weakest in South Sudan (0–1%)

Key Demographic Highlights

  • Population: ~315 million (2025 estimate)
  • Median Age: 19 years — representing a young, energetic, and opportunity-driven consumer base.
  • Urbanization: Over 30% of the population now lives in urban centers, fueling growth in retail, housing, transport, and technology sectors.
  • Mobile Penetration: Exceeds 80%, with rising smartphone adoption accelerating digital marketing and e-commerce expansion.
  • Internet Usage: Roughly 45–50% of the population is online, with Kenya and Rwanda leading in digital inclusion.
  • GDP Growth: Regional average of 5–6% annually, fueled by infrastructure, agriculture, and service sector expansion.

Consumer Landscape

East African consumers are young, aspirational, and increasingly digital. They are influenced by social media, peer communities, and mobile connectivity, creating significant opportunities for direct engagement, digital advertising, and localized brand storytelling.

This generation of consumers values authenticity, accessibility, and innovation, rewarding brands that align with their lifestyle and speak to their ambitions.
